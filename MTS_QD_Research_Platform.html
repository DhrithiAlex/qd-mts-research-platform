Multi-Colour Labelling Strategy

MTS involves simultaneous dysregulation of multiple cell populations. A 3–4 channel QD panel allows co-visualisation of all major pathological changes in a single tissue section — impossible with conventional organic dyes due to photobleaching.

MTS Biomarker Reference
Conjugation Protocol Notes
EDC/NHS Coupling
Covalent conjugation of antibodies (GFAP, NeuN, NPY) to carboxyl-functionalized QDs. QY loss ~10–20%. Optimal for InP/ZnS and CuInS₂/ZnS. NHS ester intermediate at pH 7.2.
Streptavidin–Biotin
High-affinity (K_d ~10⁻¹⁵ M) non-covalent linkage. Biotinylated primary antibody + SA-coated QD. Larger hydrodynamic size (~25 nm) limits BBB use. Excellent for ex vivo multiplex panels.
RVG-29 Peptide (In Vivo)
29-amino-acid peptide derived from rabies virus glycoprotein. Binds nAChR on BBB endothelium. Conjugated via thiol-maleimide chemistry to PEG-coated Si QDs or Ag₂S. Enables hippocampal targeting in vivo.
